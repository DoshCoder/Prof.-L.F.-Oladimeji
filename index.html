Profile Summary

Professor Lateef Folorunsho Oladimeji is the 6th substantive Vice-Chancellor of Al-Hikmah University, Ilorin, Nigeria. Appointed in July 2025, he is a distinguished Professor of Islamic Studies and a foundational member of the institution.

Current Position

Vice-Chancellor, Al-Hikmah University (appointed July 2025)

Academic Rank: Professor of Islamic Studies (attained in 2018)

Born: April 9, 1963

Educational Background

  • B.A. (Hons) Islamic Studies: University of Ilorin (1987), graduating as the best student in his department
  • M.A. & Ph.D. Islamic Studies: University of Ilorin (2005)
  • Master of Public Administration (MPA): University of Ilorin (1995)
  • PGDE: University of Ilorin (2000)

Personal Information

Origin: Hails from Oko-Ode in Ifelodun Local Government Area, Kwara State

Family: Married to Hajia Balqees Modupe Oladimeji; they have three children

Community: Active member of the Islamic community with extensive service records

Professional Fellowships

  • Fellow, Chartered Institute of Personnel Management of Nigeria (CIPMN) since 2016
  • Fellow, Chartered Public Administrators of England and Wales (2006)
  • Member of several academic and professional associations

Research Interests

  • Da'wah and Contemporary Islamic Studies
  • Peace and Conflict Studies
  • Public Administration
  • Islamic Education and Pedagogy
  • Interreligious Dialogue

External Service

Executive Secretary of the Kwara State Muslim Pilgrims Welfare Board (1995–1998 and 2002–2003)

Pioneer Staff: Joined Al-Hikmah University in 2006 as a foundational member

Consultant: Served as consultant to various educational and religious bodies

Strategic Vision: The "STRESS-FREE" Framework

Upon assuming office in July 2025, Prof. Oladimeji introduced the STRESS-FREE policy framework to guide the university's sustainable growth and development towards becoming a world-class institution.

S

Strategic Plan

Implementation of a comprehensive long-term roadmap for institutional development and excellence, aligning with national educational goals and global best practices.

T

Teamwork

Fostering a collaborative environment among academic and non-academic staff, students, and stakeholders to achieve common institutional goals.

R

Revenue Drive

Increasing internally generated revenue through innovative programs, partnerships, and investments to reduce reliance on student fees and ensure financial sustainability.

E

Expansion

Growing the university's physical infrastructure, academic programs, and student enrollment while maintaining quality standards and institutional identity.

S

Staff & Student Welfare

Prioritizing the well-being, professional growth, and development of all members of the university community through supportive policies and programs.

S

Security

Ensuring a safe and secure campus environment through modern security systems, policies, and community engagement for all students, staff, and visitors.

F

Funding

Securing diverse funding sources including grants, endowments, and partnerships to support research, infrastructure, and operational excellence.

R

Ranking

Improving the university's local and international academic standing through quality research, teaching excellence, and strategic partnerships.

E

Entrepreneurship

Integrating practical business skills and entrepreneurial mindset into the curriculum to prepare graduates for self-employment and job creation.

E

Evaluation

Establishing rigorous systems for continuous performance assessment, quality assurance, and feedback mechanisms for sustained improvement.

Additional Leadership Priorities

Internationalization

Developing global partnerships, increasing international student enrollment, and promoting cross-cultural exchange programs.

Research Excellence

Establishing research centers, increasing publication output, and securing competitive research grants.

Community Engagement

Strengthening university-community partnerships through outreach programs and social responsibility initiatives.
